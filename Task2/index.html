Phishing Awareness Training

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to steal sensitive information. In 2023, phishing remains one of the most common cyber threats, with over 90% of cyber attacks starting with a phishing email.

Key Statistics:

  • 3.4 billion fake emails are sent daily
  • 30% of phishing emails get opened
  • $17,700 lost every minute due to phishing attacks

Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

Mass-sent emails impersonating legitimate organizations.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks using personal information.

Whaling

Targeting high-profile executives.

Smishing

SMS-based phishing attacks.

How to Protect Yourself

  1. Verify Sender Identity

    Check email addresses carefully, especially for slight misspellings.

  2. Use Multi-Factor Authentication

    Add an extra layer of security beyond passwords.

  3. Hover Before Clicking

    Check link destinations by hovering over them first.

  4. Keep Systems Updated

    Regular updates help protect against known vulnerabilities.

  5. Report Suspicious Emails

    Forward suspicious emails to your IT department or spam@uce.gov.

Real-World Examples

Netflix Payment Update

Common scam claiming your Netflix account needs payment verification.

  • Generic greeting
  • Urgent action required
  • Threatening account suspension

Bank Security Alert

Fake bank security notifications about suspicious activities.

  • Requests immediate login
  • Links to fake login pages
  • Asks for full account details

Security Best Practices

Password Management

  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Use a password manager

Email Safety

  • Don't open unexpected attachments
  • Verify sender addresses carefully
  • Use spam filters
